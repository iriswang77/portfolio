3D Hologram Museum Research

Investigating the Effects of Pepper's Ghost Technology on Knowledge Acquisition and Visitor Experience

Bachelor's Thesis
University of Tübingen
2024
Cognitive Science

Project Overview

This research investigated whether Pepper's Ghost holographic technology could enhance museum visitor experiences and learning outcomes compared to traditional screen displays. The study was conducted in collaboration with the Leibniz Institute for Knowledge Media and focused on a maritime museum exhibition context.

The research addressed a critical gap in understanding how emerging display technologies impact cognitive processing, knowledge retention, and overall visitor engagement in cultural heritage settings.

Key Research Question

"Does Pepper's Ghost technology provide educational and experiential advantages over traditional screen-based displays in museum environments?"

Research Methodology

👥

Participants

50 participants (ages 19-66) recruited from diverse academic backgrounds, excluding physics/engineering to avoid prior knowledge bias.

🏛️

Experimental Setup

Controlled museum environment featuring maritime artifacts with Pepper's Ghost vs. traditional screen comparison.

📊

Measurements

Knowledge acquisition tests, cognitive load assessment, object presence evaluation, and visitor experience questionnaires.

🔬

Design

Between-subjects design with randomized assignment to ensure unbiased comparison between display methods.

Key Findings

Comprehensive analysis across multiple dimensions of user experience and learning outcomes

No Sig.
Knowledge Retention

Both display methods showed equally high information retention rates (Screen: 7.84/12, Hologram: 7.48/12)

Equal
Cognitive Load

No significant differences in extraneous, intrinsic, or germane cognitive load between conditions

Similar
Object Presence

Pepper's Ghost did not significantly enhance the perception of object presence (4.04 vs 3.84/7)

Comparable
Visitor Experience

High satisfaction scores across education, aesthetics, entertainment, and escapism dimensions

Design Implications & Insights

💡 Technology vs. Content

Visual novelty alone doesn't guarantee enhanced learning. Content quality and presentation structure remain primary factors in educational effectiveness.

🎯 Interactivity Matters

Passive viewing experiences, even with advanced display technology, may not significantly enhance engagement compared to active, interactive elements.

🌟 Environmental Factors

Lighting conditions critically impact holographic display effectiveness. Darker environments enhance the illusion and user immersion.

📈 Cost-Benefit Analysis

Museums should carefully evaluate the cost-effectiveness of premium display technologies against traditional, well-designed alternatives.
