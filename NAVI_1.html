NAVI

Autonomous Navigation for Foreign Cities

UX Design User Research Mobile App Navigation Figma

Project Overview

The Challenge

Navigating a foreign city can be overwhelming and time-consuming. Traditional navigation apps don't consider personal preferences or travel purposes, leading to generic recommendations that may not match what users actually want to discover.

The Solution

NAVI is a concept-based navigation app focused on user autonomy. It allows travelers to define their own exploration modes and priorities, making city navigation more efficient and personalized to their specific interests and goals.

My Role

As part of the T3G5 team, I contributed to concept development, user research, prototype design, and evaluation. I focused on the autonomy-centered design approach and user experience optimization.

Key Objectives

• Enable autonomous decision-making for travelers
• Provide customizable exploration modes
• Streamline route planning process
• Support diverse travel purposes (business, culture, entertainment)

Technology Stack

Figma User Research Prototyping A/B Testing

Timeline

6 weeks of intensive design and development, including concept ideation, prototype creation, user testing, and iterative refinement based on feedback.

Psychological Needs Assessment

The app successfully addressed key user needs, with Stimulation (3.83/5) and Autonomy (3.42/5) scoring highest. Users unanimously agreed that autonomy was the best-addressed need, validating our core design concept.

Key Insights & Learnings

✅ What Worked Well

  • Strong visual design received consistently positive feedback
  • Autonomy-focused concept successfully validated by all users
  • Feature set felt complete - no missing functionality reported
  • Intuitive navigation and aesthetic appeal scored highly
  • Custom mode creation resonated with user needs

⚠️ Areas for Improvement

  • Initial usability could be improved with onboarding tutorial
  • Settings split across two locations caused confusion
  • Need for current location indicator on map
  • Dark mode functionality requested by multiple users
  • Some interface elements needed clearer affordances

Design Impact & Next Steps

The evaluation confirmed our autonomy-centered approach while highlighting specific usability improvements. The app successfully balances feature richness with simplicity, achieving its goal of empowering user decision-making in foreign city navigation. Moving forward, we would implement an onboarding flow, consolidate settings, add location services, and introduce dark mode support.

Personal Reflection

This project strengthened my understanding of user-centered design principles and the importance of iterative testing. Working with psychological need frameworks like autonomy, competence, and relatedness provided valuable insights into designing for intrinsic motivation. The experience reinforced the value of balancing innovative concepts with practical usability considerations.
